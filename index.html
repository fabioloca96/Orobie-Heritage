About Me

I live in Clusone, at the foot of the Presolana.

My curiosity for this landscape doesn't stop at the trail. I read the rocks like a geologist, the meadows like a botanist, the forest floor like a forager chasing mushrooms — and the odd streambed like someone hoping to find a fossil. Every path holds more than one story.

When I'm not outdoors, I'm usually playing the baghèt — the old bagpipe of the Bergamo valleys — baking sourdough bread, or teaching electronics to high school students. All of it, somehow, comes back to these mountains.
