About Me

I live in Clusone, at the foot of the queen of the Orobie: the Presolana.

It all started with mushrooms. About ten years ago I began foraging seriously, joining the local mycological group — a network of enthusiasts and old-time experts who opened my eyes to these mountains. From there I never stopped looking around: rocks, plants, fossils, forgotten stories. Understanding what I see, putting a name to things and phenomena, makes me feel part of something bigger. Every path holds more than one story.

I'm a certified Mountain Guide (Accompagnatore di Media Montagna) — a legally recognised professional qualification, not just a hobby: it means specific training in orientation, weather reading, first aid and group management, which qualifies me to guide safely on trails and non-glacial terrain in every season. I also play the baghèt — the ancient Bergamo bagpipe — and my family raises alpacas, with whom I organise walks for families. By trade, I teach electronics in high school. All of it, somehow, comes back to these mountains.
